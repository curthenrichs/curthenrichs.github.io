During graduate school, I studied how human workers collaborate with a cobot. My colleague and I ran two experiments, Task Interdependence and pRAD, on a shared framework we called ITER, using a Universal Robots UR3e. The two studies were two halves of one effort; we each led one and stayed hands-on across both.

My work was largely technical, building the experiment setup in ROS and learning the Universal Robots platform and its Polyscope visual programming tool inside and out. On pRAD (the Operator Interface Aids study) I took the lead as experimenter and author.
